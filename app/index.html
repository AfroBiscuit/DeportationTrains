US Deportation Trains

Mapping the History of American Deportation

This project draws on a complex database drawn from US department of labor case files of people who were deported from the United States in the early 20th century. The database was developed with colleagues from Stanford University's Center for Spatial and Textual Analysis, a world-leading centre in digital humanities and the emergent field known as `spatial history.' The database records the spatial and temporal information of numerous people from around the world as they travelled to the USA, but were then captured and deported as so-called `undesirable aliens.' The information contains elements of migrants' lives prior to their arrival in the United States and within the United States; information about when, where, and why they were arrested; and information about their forced migration as deportees.
